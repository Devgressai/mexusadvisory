For many foreign nationals, particularly those who maintain business, investment, or family connections in the United States, U.S. life insurance can be a valuable financial planning tool. However, obtaining a policy is not simply a matter of applying with a U.S. carrier. Insurance companies must verify that the applicant maintains a legitimate connection—known as "nexus"—to the United States.

This requirement exists because life insurance policies issued by U.S. carriers must comply with strict regulatory and jurisdictional standards. As a result, insurers must be able to demonstrate that the insured has a credible relationship with the United States.

## What U.S. Nexus Means for Life Insurance

In the context of insurance underwriting, nexus refers to a meaningful and ongoing connection between the policyholder and the United States. This connection helps establish that issuing the policy within the U.S. regulatory system is appropriate and compliant.

Without this connection, most U.S. carriers will not approve coverage for a foreign national.

## Common Ways Foreign Nationals Establish Nexus

Many international clients naturally meet this requirement through ordinary financial or personal ties to the United States. Examples may include:

- Ownership of property in the United States
- Investment or operational involvement in a U.S. business
- Maintaining banking or financial relationships with U.S. institutions
- Spending meaningful time in the United States each year
- Holding a visa that supports ongoing U.S. activity

These connections demonstrate that the policyholder maintains a legitimate relationship with the U.S. financial system and regulatory environment.

## Additional Structural Requirements

Foreign nationals who qualify through nexus must typically satisfy a few additional requirements before a policy can be issued. These often include:

- Being physically present in the United States during the application process
- Completing standard medical underwriting
- Providing financial documentation supporting the coverage requested
- Verifying identity and compliance with international regulatory standards

While these requirements are straightforward, proper coordination is important to ensure the application process proceeds smoothly.
